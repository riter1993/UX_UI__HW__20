REDESIGN_


Our team worked with the Minneapolis non-profit "Full Cycle" to help redesign their webpage in order to break down the flow of information, as well as increase their donation engagement, all while keeping responsive web design and accessibility at the forefront of the redesign.

THE PROBLEM_

Full Cycle is a Twin Cities-based non-profit supporting housing-displaced youth through their passion for bicycles and repairs. We have observed that Full Cycleâ€™s website does not provide the user with enough information on where their donations are going.

THE SOLUTION_

We developed a responsive web page for Full Cycle that better connects the nonprofit to the community by putting their story front and center in hopes of increasing patronage and donations and to spread awareness of the homeless epidemic in Minneapolis.

USER RESEARCH_

Research was conducted over the first week of the project duration. This was mainly done through interviews, both with Full Cycle administration and potential site users. We structured an affinity diagram using our interview findings.

After organizing our data into groups we found that people were emotionally motivated when choosing a charity to give to. They want to know the story of the organization and how their donations will positively affect the community. We also found that a lack of information about the organization or where their money is allocated is an inhibitor to donating.

A Google Survey was also built and circulated through the class and team member's various social medias. The survey brought in more quantitative data for us to work with. Most importantly we found that users, on average, donate $50 or more to a charity annually.

We also found that 67.7% of people engage with a non-profit mainly through their website, confirming the need for a website redesign.
